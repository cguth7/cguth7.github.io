full BOTEC: assumptions & sources
  1. Atomic unit: 2020 census tracts (791 with centroid inside a Chicago community area). Each tract’s people are treated as living at its area-weighted centroid.
  2. Population: ACS 2016–2020 5-yr totals per tract (Cook County Dept. of Public Health mirror), male+female summed. Sums to ~2.69M, vs 2.746M in the 2020 decennial count — centroid clipping + ACS noise explains the gap.
  3. Distance: straight-line (haversine) tract centroid → station. No street network, no entrances; real walks are ~1.2–1.4× longer.
  4. Exponential weight: w = e−d/λ. Default λ = 0.5 mi ≈ a 10-minute walk; transit ridership literature usually shows steep decay past 0.5 mi for rail walk-access.
  5. Driving share: per community area from CMAP Community Data Snapshots 2023 (ACS 5-yr, table B08301-equivalent): (drove alone + carpool) ÷ commuters. Tracts inherit their community area’s share; the slider scales all areas uniformly. Commute mode ≠ all trips — treat as a proxy for car orientation.
  6. Stations: 144 current CTA ‘L’ stations (City portal export, June 2025, incl. Damen/Lake) + 4 planned Red Line Extension stations (103rd, 111th, Michigan Ave, 130th; coordinates approximated from CTA preferred-alignment maps; target ~2030).
  7. Double counting: per-station “weighted catchment” counts a tract toward every nearby station; “primary catchment” assigns each tract only to its nearest station.
  8. Sources: tract boundaries census-geojson (Census 500k cartographic files); stops & rail lines City of Chicago data portal via GitHub mirrors; population ccdph-data-sets; commute CMAP snapshots. Regenerate with build_data.py.
