Current Metrics Undervalue GWAS Research

How do we measure the return on investment from NIH-funded genomic research? Traditional approaches systematically undercount the value created.

The Citation Problem

Most ROI studies rely on patent citations to academic papers. But citations only capture explicit references—they miss when research influences innovation without direct citation.

The Spillover Problem

Current metrics only count direct outcomes. But scientific knowledge is interconnected—a discovery about one gene informs research on biologically related genes.
