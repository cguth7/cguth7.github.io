Key Messages

  • Slide 1: GWAS discoveries have a 60x spillover multiplier - for every direct gene-disease pair discovered, 60 additional pairs see increased pharmaceutical innovation through biological pathway connections.
  • Slide 2: Novel "landscape model" methodology that measures distance between gene-disease candidates using KEGG biological pathways (1/2/3-hop network distance) and MeSH disease hierarchies.
  • Why it matters for NIH: Provides evidence-based ROI assessment for genomic research investments - the true impact of GWAS is much larger than direct discoveries alone.

Data Sources

  • GWAS Catalog: 64K discoveries
  • USPTO Patents: 140M patent-gene-disease associations
  • KEGG Pathways: 30K+ genes with biological connections
  • Time span: 2000-2020 (21 years)
