The mission

On July 1 I start a month-long creator bootcamp in Berkeley about AI — including the people who think it might end the world. Instead of flying, I'm spending 20 days getting there at ground level: cornfields, canal towpaths, an 11,000-foot Rockies pass, Donner Pass, and three overnight rides on Amtrak's most scenic train. Along the way I'm interviewing farmers, railroad workers, casino dealers, and everyone in between about one thing: AI. The contrast is the whole point.

“In 10 years, AI will ______.”

Asked at diner counters, in the train lounge car, at bike shops and gas stations — all the way from Illinois farm country to the heart of AI world.

The route

Bike where it's beautiful and rideable. Train through the Plains and the Nevada desert. One westbound line — the California Zephyr — stitches it all together.

Jun 11–16 · ~260 mi

Chicago → Mt. Pleasant, Iowa bike

Out of the city on the Illinois Prairie Path, down the Fox River, then the I&M Canal towpath and old highway towns: Ottawa, Princeton, Galesburg. Cross the Mississippi at Burlington. Hammock camping along the canal, small-town diners at dawn.

Jun 16 · overnight

Mt. Pleasant → Denver zephyr

Roll the bike into the baggage car, sleep through Nebraska, wake up at the foot of the Rockies. The lounge car doubles as an interview studio.

Jun 18–22 · ~220 mi

Denver → Glenwood Springs bike

The hard part. Clear Creek Canyon to Idaho Springs, then over Berthoud Pass — 11,307 feet — and down the Colorado River headwaters through Kremmling, the canyon-rim gravel of Trough Road, and the Glenwood Canyon bike path. Summit by noon or the lightning decides for you.

Jun 22 · afternoon

Glenwood Springs → Salt Lake City zephyr

Through Ruby Canyon and the Utah desert while my legs file a formal complaint.

Jun 23–24

Salt Lake City rest

Two days with family. Laundry, real beds, bike check, and the “someone who knows me explains why this trip is ridiculous” interview.

Jun 24 · overnight

Salt Lake City → Reno zephyr

The Nevada desert in June is 100°F of nope. This is the smart hop.

Jun 25–28 · ~235 mi

Reno → Berkeley bike

Up the Truckee River, over Donner Pass on the old Highway 40 — yes, that Donner Pass; the first group to attempt this route famously did not make it — then a 6,000-foot descent to Auburn, the car-free American River Parkway into Sacramento, through Davis (bike capital of America), and across the Benicia Bridge to the Bay.

Jun 28 · arrival

Berkeley, CA made it

Roll straight to the bootcamp's front door with 20 days of stories, a camera roll full of strangers' predictions, and legs made of granite.

The rules of the road

  • Beat the weather. Riding done by early afternoon in the heat; over the mountain passes before the lightning shows up at noon.
  • One video a day. Filmed from the saddle, the diner counter, and the lounge car. No exceptions, including today.
  • Powered by the holy trinity: peanut butter, bananas, and Oreos. Roughly 4,500 calories a day, mostly vegan, occasionally desperate.
  • Sleep where there are trees. Hammock most nights — canal towpaths, national forest pines — with a motel shower every few days for everyone's benefit.
  • The train forgives nothing. One Zephyr a day, each direction. Miss it and you wait 24 hours. There are exactly two buffer days.
